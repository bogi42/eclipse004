# Frontrunner

It doesn't take me long to find the next breach in the city walls. It's as I've feared:
One more grenadier with one of those strange energy weapons has torn another hole into the
wall. Lucky for me---and everyone behind me---, those weapons seem to be rare. My knowledge
about adversarial tech is limited, if I'm being honest, but it seems like those kinds of
weapons can't be mass-produced.

Or maybe not simply anyone can handle them, and it takes a special boy to actually use
them.

So far, not a single soul has noticed me, running atop the wall. I feel conflicted about
it, but I use an Umbral Volley to quickly kill the man *and* destroy his equipment at the
same time. I just hope no one has seen me use the spell: I would rather not be taken for
an enemy *again*---I've already had enough of those shenanigans during the early days of
the integration. All that mistrust, and I'm weary and tired of it.

At least, back then, my shadow powers were somewhat of a mystery to the people. Something
they didn't recognize. Fat chance of that happening now---the attackers have clearly
demonstrated the shadow attribute to the citizens of Hope. Thanks a lot for hat, ass hats!

But the defenders are actually too busy with---well, defending their city, to notice what
I'm doing. A battle is raging this side of the wall. I grip my gun and go to work, careful
not to let any dark powers slip. My twelve-shooter is the only reliable attack I can use,
but after all the upgrades, I can pump Mana into it really fast to regrow my ammunition.
I don't expect long pauses between shots. At least not I space them out a bit---if I go
trigger-happy, then I might have to take a few moments before I can shoot again.

Both sides, attackers and defenders, are quite surprised when I jump into the fray. My
first two bullets rip apart a pair of enemies while I'm still airborne. The kickback of my
revolver has reached a point that would have broken the wrist of any pre-integration
human, even former Californian governors with thick muscles. But to me? I barely register
the kick.

It is a wondrous point: How do all those attribute values actually scale? Is there
a noticeable difference between ten and eleven points in the Body attribute? And if so,
what about the difference between eight and nine hundred points? Does that scale linearly,
or are there some other concepts at play here?

*Stop overthinking and get back into the fight, Alaric!*

Right, right, of course. My first two victims drop down dead, and just like last time,
there's no visible blood---well, not red blood, anyway. Our adversaries’ bodies pump out
a dark liquid, ranging between midnight grey and black. It's viscous, like tar. *Is that
how the lungs of long-time smokers look like on the inside?* One has to wonder.

Quite a few people around me are audibly surprised, but at least I no longer look like the
grim reaper himself, allowing my fellow citizens to accept me as their backup. That
superficiality is grating on me, but at the same---that's what I wanted to achieve, right?

But now I'm in the midst of the fighting, and thankfully can no longer spare my thoughts
on the matter. A real hail of arrows try to hit me, but I just move my upper body the bare
minimum I need to dodge them. They harmlessly pass me by. Well, harmlessly to me; their
flight path is cut short as they strike the defendant behind me. Seems to me that guy has
bad reflexes and not too much armour. I can hear three *thuds* as the arrows drill into
the man, followed up by a loud crush as the poor man falls on his back.

*Crap.*

The archer remains unemotional. From his perspective, the attack was probably successful,
too---he might not have hit his intended target, but he did hit *a* target. I guess he's
happy with this consolidation prize. I growl at him and put a bullet between his eyes.
Without wasting time, I turn around and stoop over the defender. I rip the arrows from his
chest, making him scream in anguish. Immediately, I stop the bleeding and heal his wounds
with Healing Light. It works, but the poor man is rather pale; I'm not sure whether he can
keep on fighting. One of the other soldiers grabs him under the arms and pulls him to the
side, taking him out of the fight for now.

A melee combatant jumps at me, wildly swinging a great sword. His weapon descends upon me
like a judgement. Oddly enough, that thought strikes a spark within me. There's something
there, on a conceptual level, that speaks to me. I almost stumble because of it, but with
a snarl, I pull my head out of these thoughts. My left arm performs half a circle, pushing
the blade on the flat side to miss me. With a loud crunch, the sword scratches into the
stone to my side. Sparks fly as the tip drills into the street.

As my left arm pushes the blade to the side, my right hand rises. It's one of the combat
manoeuvres I've trained with Hormund, back on Samarund. He taught me knife combat, but the 
technique is applicable to the barrel of my gun as well. Its length nicely fits the blade
length of a combat knife. Here I was, thinking all that technique was going to rot since
I've lost the use of the Eidolon in its Kris mode. Apparently, I was mistaken.

It's not the tip of a knife that drills into the underside of my enemies lower jaw, it's
the top of the barrel. And it doesn't really drill into the flush, it just pushes a bit.
But unlike the knife, the barrel has no need to enter my enemy’s body---after all, that's
what bullets are there for. I only have to move my finger, and his brain splatters all
around. Wet chunks hit me and the people around me.

A bit tasteless and morbid, maybe, but effective nonetheless.

It's as if my shot turned into a secret signal to finally start the mayhem, as all hell
breaks loose around me. Attacks, counters, and limbs fly. Perhaps I just imagine things,
but it feels like my kill shot has put the whole fight into overdrive.

Swords whoosh, arrows whistle through the air. A bolt of dark lightning crashes into one
of the defenders next to me. *That thing almost looks like my old Shadow Bolt, back when
I was below the tenth level.* This bolt, though, was shot from the barrel of a futuristic
gun. It looks like a shotgun that was put through a process to make it look more
cyberpunk.

One more puzzle piece tries to snug into the picture: Yes, the attackers have shadows, but
it seems like they are not fully controlling those powers. They need tech to bridge the
gap between their shadows and their abilities. It's almost as if they are not really
attuned to shadows, rather than being attuned to an over-shadowed version of something
else. Like a water attunement has turned into an attunement of shady water. They seem to
be unable to use the shadows directly, although every spell and skill they use is tainted
by the shadows.

So that bolt was done by a strange piece of technology. A scream of anguish rises next to
me, and its volume and intensity is such that they dominate the whole scenery for just
a moment.

That moment ends with the clack-clack sound of a shotgun, as the attacker is preparing to
shoot once more. *Oh no, you don't!* I use a Shadow Step, hoping against hope that this
ability is not clearly recognizable as a shadow skill. I reappear behind the gunner, my
barrel pressed into the back of his head. Then his head ceases to exist with a loud bang.

All around me, shocked faces stare at me. I hope it's due to the brutality of the attack,
and not due to the usage of my skill. Yeah, maybe it's the brutality---then again, this is
war. I hope no one expected me to pelt the enemy with balls of cotton. But it might just
be the teleport.

*Crap!* I have to tone down my skill usage even more.

It could be it's recognizable as a shadow skill. To avoid antagonizing the defenders even
more, I pass the chance to teleport back. Instead, I duck and run, while my gun does the
talking. And it talks about fire, war, rage, and death. Not sure anyone is listening.

Around me, attackers drop like birds. But they don't let up, instead they try to drown me
with their bodies. I feel like the ball in a game of American Football, when all the
players lump together. That does mostly nothing to me, at least regarding damage: They
can't really hurt me. 

But they can trap me under a barrage of bodies, and they do. I can't even use my hand
holding the gun, unless I plan to shoot the ground, in which case I'm golden. Well, *this*
would be the perfect moment to use a Shadow Nova. The energy within me starts to hum and
forms the necessary pattern even before I finish my thought. It's like a reflex.

But I can't afford to act without thinking. Shadow Nova is, undoubtedly, one of the
flashiest shadow spells in my repertoire, along there with Umbral Judgement. I can't use
it while in the midst of all the defenders. I'm not even sure they won't be hurt by it!
And how would they react to such an attack?

As I mull this over, one of the attackers stares directly into my face. Contrary to my
expectations, there's no anger or fury in his eyes. Not even hate, in fact: nothing. He's
got dead eyes, if I've ever seen any, and all in all, he looks more like a dummy than
a human being.

The only movement in his face comes from the shadows under his skin---writhing, dancing,
moving all around. Like eels in a pond, constantly on the move. Here and there, they seem
to press against the skin from underneath, bulging the face for a bit. I have to keep onto
my last meal with all my might, just looking at that.

Is that what shadows do to humans? In the midst of all this corrupted human beings, I can
feel the pressure the shadows exude. It's not just the collected weight of the people
pressing me into the ground, it's also the spiritual pressure of all the surrounding
shadows. It's pressing directly onto my brain instead of my body.

There's a sweet temptation behind all of that, and it's not even the first time I feel it.
The first time, that was in the library when I discovered the scroll that granted me
visions from the System’s birth. Although, with all I know now, that wasn't really the
start. It was more like the start of the modern revision of the System, not its first
iteration.

And there was someone in that vision, someone I believe to be the Adversary. He talked to
me, made me an offer that I absolutely had to refuse. But the temptation back then was
strong. It's like a siren’s call, emanating from the shadows---within and without.

It's that siren’s call I'm feeling right now.

Cold sweat forms on my forehead. All those warnings I received about light and shadow,
about the balance---have I really heeded them? What if I didn't? Could it be that me still
using shadow powers slowly pulls me closer and closer to the source of the temptation? 

Am I becoming one of the bad guys?

This insight changes a lot. Refusing to use my shadow powers suddenly becomes more than
just a way to not scare the defenders. Instead, it turns into a core pillar of my
identity. After all, can I stay human if I keep using those powers? I can feel my throat
constricting in panic.

What is the price I'm paying for all my powers?

Screams and calls pull me out of my lethargic thoughts. Suddenly, there's movement in the
mass of bodies above me as the defenders arrive on the scene and start pulling them off
me. I'm lying helpless on the ground, watching in awe at the scene unfolding in front of
me.

They have come to rescue me. Of course, they don't know that I'm basically in no danger,
not with all the resistances I possess. They also don't know how easy it would be for me
to kill all the enemies in one, fell swoop. That would probably kill the defenders as
well, in the same instant. They must never know that.

But they do not know, and they have come to rescue me. There's a warm feeling spreading in
my chest.

Not for long, though---it's soon accompanied by negative emotions because the rescue
mission is not going according to plan. There's a lot of close fighting going on, and more
than one defender goes down with serious wounds. Healing Light has a cool down of almost
two minutes left. Its smaller cousin, Healing Touch, only has a total cool down of thirty
seconds. I could use it right now---if only I could reach one of the poor guys.

The Touch in the spell’s name is not for decoration, you know? I'm not a dedicated healer,
at best I'm a front pig carrying a first aid kit. Only right now, I can't even reach the
kit---my hands are tied, so to speak.
