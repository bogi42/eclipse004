# Arrival in difficult Times

Outside the big panorama window lies the City of Hope, like a decorative carpet underneath
my feet. I have to throw myself once more unto the breach, but before I do that, I take
a moment to digest the view. The colourful mix of different, architectural styles is still
a wild clash, as always, but it is also something familiar.

The first time I entered this city–nameless, back then–I was a stranger. I wasn't even
allowed to live here. Briefly, I wonder if that restriction is still there; plenty of new
people have moved in since then. Back then, it was because I wasn't a part of the
respective tutorial group, but that restriction should have long since been lifted.

It doesn't matter, anyway; more important is the fact that I'm no longer a stranger here.
Direct in front of me is the arena where I was forced to fight against my girlfriend
(before we were an item), at a time everyone feared me. First I was a monster to them,
then became a hero when we saved the city from the Adderkin.

Now? I'm relegated back to being a stranger. There are so many new people here that have
never before seen me. It's a little strange, but I'm also giddy about it: It's a new
beginning. A chance to prove to everyone that I belong.

I can only hope to prove it once and for all.

Being rejected by my fellow humans–and then again by the orcs on Samarund–has left
a bitter taste on my tongue. I can still feel the bitterness in me rising when I remember
those times. With it comes a doubt, a devil that whispers to me that I will never belong.

*You don't belong to us.* Now here's a sentence I've heard fairly regularly, especially
during my teenager years. But here and now? I have a feeling that belonging is no longer
a pipe dream, but something that's achievable for me. I just can't mess this up.

A clearing of the throat sounds behind me. »I wish you'd look at *me* the way you look at
the city,« Lyra grins at me. Then her expression turns serious. »The siege?«

I nod. Seems it's about time to save my new home and prove that I'm a friend, not a foe.
What better chance is there to do this than to play the guardian? My eye travels across
the city and arrives at the wall–and the plain in front of it. For the first time since my
return, I frown.

There's a wild horde out there. The plain outside the city walls is packed with soldiers,
who seem to be fighting for a place to simply stand there. How many are out there? Maybe
this really *is* the wild horde. Where's Genghis Khan?

With a dramatic flick of my wrist, my Soul Scythe appears in my hand. A cloak weaved of
shadows comes into existence around me, turning me into the grim reaper. I look like an
album cover from Blue Öyster Cult.

Man, I miss music. The multiverse must be full of bad people because so far, I have yet
to stumble upon a streaming service for music.

All the members of the council behind me gasp audibly for ear, and there's a loud *thud*
as the gentleman next to the already unconscious woman loses his consciousness and puts his
forehead to the table. I can't help but to smirk a little bit: During the early
integration, I regularly lost consciousness. Of course, I never did so simply because
I looked at a strong monster–I usually had better reasons. 

I do wonder, though, what qualifies these people to have become members of the council.
Personal strengths most certainly is not the reason. But having a look at those people,
I can see they're all afraid. They glance at me like I've come to kill their firstborn
children. Except for Lyra and Brok, of course.

At this point, something totally unexpected happens, as I receive a message from the
System. It's a rare one, at least in the last months:

::: line-block
[**New Title:** Now you've done it]{.u}

It really happened: Your face frightened someone so much that they lost consciousness. Do
you like beating up the weak?

*+ 5 base soul damage*
:::

What? Fuck you, too, System! I've done nothing to earn that. At least not that I'm aware.
And why should people be frightened by me, anyway?

Although: The guy in the suit is the only one of the bunch currently not sitting down, but
the way his knees move makes me think he'd better sit down. Sweat is running down his
face, and his hands are visibly shaking. There's definitely fear there.

Brok, on the other hand, yawns. Maybe he's just unimpressed, having already seen my reaper
form, or he's bored. Perhaps a bit of both. Lyra, on the other hand, says nothing. She just
looks at me, one eyelid drawn high. It's a look that promises there's a *talk* in the near
future waiting for me.

I grin at her, only to realize that she can't see my face because of the hood. Why the
System chose to outfit me this way while using my Soul Scythe is something I'll never
understand. Oh, well, can't change it, anyway.

Without further ado, I teleport outside the window and look towards the city walls.
There's a section with plenty of magical fireworks. Looks like there's a massive
conflict raging, and also like the perfect place to start.

I grip my scythe harder and move that way.
