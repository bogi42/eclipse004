# Foreword and Recap

Thank you for your continued interest in my little post-apocalyptic world! The fact you’re
reading this means a lot to me, and I am very grateful to you for giving an indie author
like me a chance. ***Thank you!***

By now, I have established that I’ll be using stat dumps rather sparingly throughout the
text. The numbers are still there (though *something* is gonna change in the future her,
but no spoilers). I just don’t want to bloat the text and distract you with the numbers,
so I’ll use them partially. Alright?

So, let’s get into our recap of the first three books!

## Book 1: All The Shadows

The world doesn't end with a bang, but with a blue screen. For Alaric, it means being
transported into a new reality fraught with danger, coming from the Adversary—and his
fellow humans. Alaric decides to grow and soon discovers that the line between human and
monster can wear pretty thin at times. He powers up with a profession called »Bone
Engraver« thanks to a tainted Dungeon and makes a few new enemies, but finds a new friend
in Aury, a little dragon he rescues under strange circumstances from another Dungeon.

While battling Adversarial forces and growing his powers, he comes into contact with other
survivors of the integration. Not all is well as he soon makes an enemy out of Caius,
a pompous healer with a pretty arrogant streak. Alaric fights against the invasions and
manages to drive them back, but is soon shunted by the rest of Earth’s population. Caius
manipulates the Dawson sisters and tries to get one of them to fight him. Alaric manages
to coax the truth out of Caius, driving the man out of Earth’s first city. But that is
just the start of his problems as a red zone threats to break and unleash a deadly terror
on the city and its population.

## Book 2: A Glimpse Beyond

Searching for more power before the giant Adderkin razes humanity’s only city, Alaric once
more goes Dungeon delving. He finds a new friend, a dwarven prince named Garrik who
travels home to Earth with Alaric. He also meets a mysterious Kraken in the Dungeon who
implants something in his brain, and learns a second profession.

During the battle for the City of Hope, Alaric manages to burn away the Kraken’s influence
as well as establishing himself as a hero, whether he likes it or not. He also finds love
in the aftermath with Lyra who joins him and Garrik on a trip to Earth’s very own Mega
Dungeon. This precious resource has been tainted as well, and our heroes set out to
cleanse the Dungeon.

They manage to do just that, powering up on the way. But it’s far from smooth
sailing---not only learns Alaric a bit more about those mysterious Kraken powers (and the
fact that there is not only a System, but an Interface, too, and both are entwined), he
also meets an entity that calls himself the Recordkeeper, who insists that Alaric is
already dead.

Since there is no rest for the wicked, cleaning the Mega Dungeon only leads to a small
break for our heroes. The remaining (and surviving) population of Earth returns from their
Tutorial, founding new cities. A few misogynistic guys try to coax the City of Hope out of
Elara Dawson’s hands, but find that dealing with Alaric is way more than they bargained
for.

And then the invasion of Earth starts in earnest, all over the planet. Alaric manages to
close the night elf incursion to the City of Hope, but just as he closes the portal, he
and Aury are sucked in and thrown to the home planet of the Night Elves…

## Book 3: New Horizons

Shortly after arriving on a planet called Samarund, Aury and Alaric are forcefully split
up as the little dragon is kidnapped (or dragonnapped). Alaric himself is captured by
slavers and removed from his System access by very advanced technology. While that is
initially bad, it soon turns out into a blessing in disguise: Over the course of the
story, this allows Alaric to learn more about the System, the Interface and his personal
hacking skills.

He meets Brok, an orc paladin. They escape together, and while Brok starts out wary of
Alaric, they eventually become friends. That doesn't happen over night, and before they
really become friends, Alaric once more turns into a solo adventurer. Disguising himself
as a slave, he infiltrates the slavers and uses already existing intrigues within the
Grimaldy family to create a lot of chaos.

He also discovers that the Grimaldy family is in league wit the Adversary when he stumbles
upon a machine designed to harvest the free will of its victims. Alaric soon learns that
this free will and the Aether he uses for his own hacking skills are very interchangeable.
They are both the same resource---something that the Recordkeeper keeps calling
*Authority*. 

Alaric accidentally overloads his own System with the master load of harvested Authority,
triggering an update to his own System. It takes a while, but he unlocks his Soul
attribute as his fourth attribute, getting closer to the original System. Later on, he
learns more about how the System came to be and what actually sits in the center of the
Multiverse.

Before that happens, though, Alaric and his somewhat shaky alliance with the Orcs defeat
the Grimaldy family, thereby killing an Adversarial emissary and stealing his starship.
(Alaric will later on install a mind clone of himself as the AI of this ship.)

Together with Brok, he searches for Aury---and finds an ancient dragon named Samaretz who
has taken a liking to Aury. She even trained the little rascal! And she sheds some light
on Velarash and his nightelves. 

Sadly, the old dragon is rather nefarious and lying, thereby breaking Aury’s little heart.
Alaric beats Velarash, but can't kill him (or Samaretz), at least not without sacrificing
the whole planet of Samarund. Instead, he strikes a deal with the orcs to safe keep
Velarash and the old dragon. What could possibly go wrong?

Together with Brok, Aury, his new starship and Soul attribute, Alaric travels back to
Earth, only to find himself entering a civil war: The shadow synchronicity has spread, and
not in a good way. Led by Caius, a part of humanity has joined the Adversary.

Luckily, Alaric has a new Scythe that needs to be thoroughly tested …
